About Me

I am a Physics Ph.D. candidate at Harvard University specializing in high energy theory. My advisor is Xi Yin. I've been working on conformal field theory and string theory, and my recent work is on formalization of QFT using Lean.

I'm also actively involved in robotics research, focusing on tactile sensing and imitation learning. I also have hands-on experience with electronics and FPGA systems.

Current Research

Tactile Data for Retargeting in Imitation Learning

This project focuses on improving robotic manipulation. I am researching ways to incorporate tactile data from human demonstrations to enhance the retargeting process for robotic grippers. As part of this work, I designed a custom tactile sensing glove to capture high-fidelity contact data.

Formalization and QFT

I am working on formulating Euclidean QFT axioms using the Lean theorem-proving language, assisted by LLMs. My work involves constructing proofs for generalized free fields satisfying the O-S Axioms.

Anomalous Dimension For Multi-trace Operators in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM

The project aims to compute tree-level anomalous dimensions of multi-trace operators in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang‚ÄìMills theory using a Hamiltonian perturbation approach. This approach is intended to simplify calculations, clarify multi-trace operator mixing, and provide a more transparent understanding of the large-N spectrum beyond current correlator-based limits.
